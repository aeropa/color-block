# The Evolution of Tetris

## Origins (1984)

Tetris was created by Soviet software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in June 1984 while working at the Dorodnitsyn Computing Centre of the Soviet Academy of Sciences in Moscow. The game was named from the Greek prefix "tetra-" (meaning four) and tennis, Pajitnov's favorite sport.

## Early Development and Soviet Era

- **1984**: First version created on the Electronika 60, a Soviet computer
- **1985**: Adapted for IBM PC and Soviet home computers like the VK Spectrum and BK-0010
- **1988**: Tetris licensing rights acquired by Spectrum HoloByte for Western release
- **1989**: Nintendo secured exclusive console rights for Game Boy, releasing Tetris there

## The Console Wars Era

- **1989**: Tetris becomes the best-selling game of the year on Game Boy
- **1990**: Arcade version released by Atari Games
- **1991**: Tetris on NES released by Nintendo (despite earlier disputes)
- **1996**: Tetris 3D and Tetrisphere introduced new visual perspectives

## Modern Era and Revival

- **2001**: Tetris Worlds popularized the "goal-based" modern Tetris
- **2005**: Tetris: The Grand Master series brought competitive Tetris to arcades in Japan
- **2006**: Tetris Effect created a revolutionary synesthetic experience
- **2019**: Tetris 99 brought battle royale to Tetris

## The Tetris Company Era

After the Soviet Union dissolved, the licensing rights became complicated. In 1996, Henk Rogers (who had secured the console rights for Game Boy) and Robert Stein formed **The Tetris Company** to manage the brand. They acquired the licensing rights from Soviet organizations and began overseeing all Tetris products.

## Competitive Tetris

- **2006**: Classic Tetris World Championships began in Portland
- **2010**: Tetris Online Japan launched competitive leaderboards
- Modern Tetris emphasizes speed, efficiency, and competitive play

## Key Innovations Over the Years

| Era | Innovation |
|-----|------------|
| 1984-1989 | Original gameplay, piece rotation system |
| 1990s | 3D variants, puzzle modes |
| 2000s | Modern "bag" randomization, soft/hard drop |
| 2010s | Battle royale, VR implementations |
| 2020s | Continued innovation with new platforms |

## Legacy

Tetris remains one of the most recognizable and influential video games in history, having sold over 200 million copies worldwide. Its simple yet addictive gameplay has transcended generations and platforms, making it a timeless classic in gaming.
